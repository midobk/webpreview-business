The Chair

A shop that still looks like a shop.

Barber Shop sits on Belmont Street, a few blocks from the water. It’s the kind of place where the radio is on low, the clippers are loud, and the chair remembers you.

You walk in, you get a nod, you sit down. There’s no booking app to wrestle with, no scrolling through service tiers. You tell us what you want, and we cut it.

That’s the whole pitch.

What we keep on the shelf

Three things, done properly.

No product lines, no memberships, no loyalty app. A straight razor, a hot towel, and a chair that doesn’t spin when you don’t ask it to.

i.

The straight razor

Honoured on the leather strop before every shave. The edge is checked twice, the blade is changed weekly, and the line is set with a hand you can’t rush.

ii.

The hot towel

Steamed, wrung, tested on the wrist. Two minutes on the face, cold towel to close. The shave feels different when the prep is real.

iii.

The chair

Vintage leather, brass footrest, and a hydraulic that holds. You’ll spend twenty minutes in it, and you’ll leave looking like the same person — just tidier.

Find the chair
