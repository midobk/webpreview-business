The shop

Same chair. Same razor. New hands.

Gilles took the shop forward the year the sign out front needed repainting. He didn't change much. The lather is still hot, the radio is still turned just loud enough, and the same straight razor sits on the same marble counter by the register.

What you notice first is the quiet — a third-generation shop doesn't need to explain itself. The walk-ins keep coming, and a few of them bring their kids now too.

"If you leave looking like someone else, I haven't done my job. You leave looking like you, only tidier."
